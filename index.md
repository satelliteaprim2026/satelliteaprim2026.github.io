# 2026 Asia-Pacific Regional IAU Satellite Meeting (APRIM2026)

## Precision Engineering for Space Exploration: Robotics, Autonomy and Instrumentation for Lunar, Planetary and Small Body Missions

**Organized by:**  
*State Key Laboratory of Ultra-precision Machining Technology, Research Centre for Deep Space Explorations (RCDSE), and Department of Aeronautical and Aviation Engineering, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University*

---

### 📌 Introduction

The APRIM 2026 Satellite meeting is a one-day satellite meeting in conjunction with the Asia-Pacific Regional IAU Meeting (APRIM2026) to be held in Hong Kong on May 3, 2026. This satellite meeting will focus on the intersection of precision engineering, space robotics, autonomous systems, and astronomical research, with particular emphasis on lunar, planetary, and small body exploration missions.

---

### 📅 Meeting Details

* **Title:** Precision Engineering for Space Exploration: Robotics, Autonomy and Instrumentation for Lunar, Planetary and Small Body Missions
* **Date:** May 3, 2026 (Sunday, one day before APRIM2026 main meeting)
* **Venue:** Room M1603 and M1607, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (HK PolyU)
* **Expected Participants:** 80-90 delegates

---

### 🎯 Themes and Topics

The meeting will focus on the following key themes:
1. Space Robotics and Mechatronics
2. Autonomous Systems for Space Exploration
3. Precision Engineering for Space Instrumentation
4. Lunar and Planetary Exploration Technologies
5. Small Body Exploration Challenges
